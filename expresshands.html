ExpressHands

AI-Powered ASL Translation Interface

A mobile app concept that visualizes real-time American Sign Language (ASL) translation using AI. Designed with accessibility and inclusivity at its core.

Project Overview

ExpressHands is a conceptual mobile application that bridges the communication gap between hearing and deaf communities through AI-powered sign language recognition and translation. The project focused on creating an intuitive, accessible interface that could interpret facial expressions and gestures to produce emotionally accurate translations.

The Problem

  • No ASL Translating Tools - mostly learning apps
  • Real conversations are still inaccessible
  • Deaf users face health, social, and emotional barriers due to miscommunication

Design Process

The design process involved extensive research into accessibility standards, user interviews with members of the deaf community, and iterative prototyping in Figma. Key considerations included:

  • Real-time gesture recognition accuracy
  • Intuitive visual feedback systems
  • Accessible features
  • Emotional context preservation in translations

Key Features

Two-way conversation flow

ASL users and non-ASL users can communicate with each other

Facial expression & tone detection

Integrated AI that detects one's emotions to interpret the accurate meanings they wish to express

Personalized profiles

Data collected to generate one's unique identity for tone and expression

Saved chat history

View previous conversations with others

100% mobile-first

Only app is needed
